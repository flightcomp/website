Glide computing

We design algorithms. The algorithms running into glide computers that proccess the data obtained from the different sensors to give you information about your climb/descent rate, glide ratio, wind speed and direction, thermal drift, etc. All this information makes you fly safer and better.

This algorithms are behind all glide computers in the market. From glide computers based on specific hardware, to glide computer software running on general purpose hardware like tablets, phones, e-readers.

We think that a more in-depth understanding of the way these algorithms work will help you correctly use this information in your xc and competition flights.

Unreliability of TE variometers in horizontally moving air

The Total Energy variometer of a glider takes into account the dynamic energy exchanges with the atmosphere. Unfortunately glider pilots take them erroneously as resulting from an effective vertical speed of the air. Consequently, their indications of dynamic energy exchanges are gathered together and delayed.
