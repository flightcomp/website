Vario computing

Roberto Chao

If you have ever shopped around for a vario then you have most likely been overwhelmed by all the features and terminology used in the manufacturer descriptions. This article is going to simplify some of the most useful product features and terminology used to describe whats under the hood of these flight instruments.

Vario averager

The vario averager is an extra functionality of some variometers that helps when you are dealing with broken thermals. As the name already indicates, this function calculates the climb rate average over a period of time. Although you were sinking, the variometer would continue beeping, probably in a somewhat different manner, until the average climb rate turned to be negative. It's the same as saying: "until the altitude gain during the interval turns negative"

How do we choose the length of this time interval? If you're turning into a thermal the time interval should be equal to the time spent in doing a full turn. The vario will encourage you to keep turning if the altitude gain in a complete turn is positive and leave if we lose altitude before completing the turn. 

When the thermal reaches an inversion layer climb rate is weaker or doesn't exist and you turn wide. A long time interval would will encourage you to stay. On the other hand when the thermal is small and strong, you turn tight and a long time interval will prevent you from detect in time when you fall out of the thermal.

Thus to make things clear the interval should vary depending on the turn radius which is not the case in most modern flight instruments.
